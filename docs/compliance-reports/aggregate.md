## How to interpret

The cross-framework headline is informational only — it averages applicable-control rates
across {len(all_rows)} disparate frameworks of widely differing scope and weight. **For any
specific compliance objective, read the per-framework report**, not the aggregate number.
PCI DSS v4.0 has 148 applicable controls; GDPR has 5 — they are not comparable on a single
PASS-rate axis.

The honest signals from this aggregate are:

1. **Zero genuine FAILs in the manual auditor scope** (ASVS L3, Agentic AI, LLM Top 10, LLM
   Governance). The auditor verdict is GREEN-WITH-OPEN-ITEMS at v2.23.2.
2. **ACS automated FAILs are predominantly heuristic file_exists / coverage_check misses**,
   many addressable by adding standard documentation files (asset_inventory.md, postmortem
   directory, BAA template) or refining a single regex; some indicate genuine v2.24.0
   backlog candidates. They are reported as FAIL honestly per `feedback_no_fake_compliance_docs.md`.
3. **Three frameworks at >95% automated rate** (ISO 27001, NIST CSF 2.0, SOC 2 Type II) —
   reflects mature documentation + code-level baseline.
4. **Largest improvement targets:** OWASP Web Top 10 (37.5%) and OWASP API Top 10 (42.9%) —
   note these are the **automated subset only**; the parallel manual evidence packs at
   `Internal/Compliance/yashigani/v2.23.2/owasp/` carry PASS-WITH-COMPENSATING verdicts at
   10/10 and 9/10 respectively. The automated rate reflects ACS regex-coverage thresholds
   that the manual pack accepts via compensating-control verdicts.
